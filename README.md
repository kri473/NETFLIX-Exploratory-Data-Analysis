# NETFLIX-Exploratory-Data-Analysis
Netflix Content Analysis ProjectProject OverviewThis project performs an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the Netflix dataset to uncover trends in movies and TV shows available on the platform from 2009 to 2021. The analysis focuses on data cleaning, content distribution across genres and countries, and how content characteristics like duration and ratings have evolved over time.  Key FeaturesData Cleaning: Handled missing values in categorical columns using mode imputation and converted date strings into datetime objects.  Outlier Removal: Identified and removed outliers in the release_year column using the Interquartile Range (IQR) method to focus on modern content trends.  Genre Analysis: Visualized the most popular genres, identifying that Dramas, International Movies, and Documentaries are the most frequent.  Geographical Insights: Mapped content concentration, highlighting the United States and India as the top content producers.  Temporal Trends: Analyzed how Netflix's library peaked between 2018 and 2019.  Content Evolution: Tracked the shift toward mature content (TV-MA) and the gradual decline in average movie duration over the years.  Dataset DescriptionThe dataset contains information about 8,807 titles, including:  Type: Movie or TV ShowDirector and CastCountry of OriginRelease Year and Date AddedRating and DurationListed In (Genres)Technologies UsedPython: Core programming language.  Pandas: For data manipulation and cleaning.  Matplotlib & Seaborn: For creating statistical visualizations.  Scipy: For statistical analysis (skewness).  Key InsightsLibrary Growth: Netflix content additions were low until 2014, followed by a sharp surge that peaked in 2018 with over 1,000 new titles.  Rating Distribution: The catalog is heavily dominated by mature content (TV-MA), followed by TV-14.  Shortening Durations: The average movie length has steadily declined from approximately 109 minutes in 2009 to around 92 minutes by 2020, suggesting a shift toward concise storytelling.  Top Genres: Documentaries and Stand-Up Comedy saw significant growth around 2017.  Conclusions & RecommendationsConclusion: Netflix prioritizes movies over TV shows, with a strong focus on adult and teen audiences in the US and Indian markets.  Recommendation: To increase engagement, Netflix could further diversify its international TV show catalog and explore niche genres to reach wider audiences.
